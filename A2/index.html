Introduction

Preference, often spelled Préférence and frequently referred to as "Preference," is a Central and Eastern European 10-card plain-trick game with bidding, played by three players with a 32-card Piquet deck, and probably originating in early 19th century Austria, becoming the second most popular game in Vienna by 1980. It also took off in Russia where it was played by the higher echelons of society, the regional variant known as Preferans being still very popular in that country, while other variants are played from Lithuania to Greece. This classic trick-taking card game challenges players' strategic thinking and card-playing finesse. Often played with four participants forming partnerships, Preference involves bidding for contracts and executing well-timed tactics to win tricks containing valuable cards. The game's blend of intricate card rankings, trump suits, and bidding strategies creates a dynamic and engaging experience, making Preference a beloved choice for those seeking an intellectually stimulating card game.
